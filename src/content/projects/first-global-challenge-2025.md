## Challenge

The 2025 challenge required a robot capable of handling multiple game elements while remaining stable, serviceable and strategically useful during matches. The system needed to collect balls, store and feed them reliably, launch them towards scoring zones and climb using a rope-based mechanism.

## Technical contribution

The robot was approached as a set of coordinated subsystems rather than one monolithic build:

- A drivetrain designed for predictable movement and match control
- An intake for collecting game elements from the field
- A feeder path for moving balls into the scoring mechanism
- A shooter capable of controlled launches
- A climber designed around torque, balance and mechanical safety
- Guides and servo locks that supported repeatable positioning

## Mentoring approach

Students were taken through requirement interpretation, mechanism comparison, CAD-based planning, prototyping, assembly, testing and iterative improvement. Technical decisions were discussed in terms of trade-offs so that the team understood why a mechanism worked, not merely how to reproduce it.

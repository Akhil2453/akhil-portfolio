## Context

The B.Tech in Design Engineering is being developed around a central principle: learners should not encounter design only as a theory subject. Design must shape how they observe, analyse, build, test and communicate throughout the programme.

## Scope of work

My work brings together curriculum development, teacher preparation and assessment design. This includes reviewing course structures, aligning lesson plans with approved syllabi, converting conventional classroom topics into activity-based sessions, and building evaluation approaches that capture both process and outcomes.

## Current initiatives

### Design Spark

A pre-academic engagement journey that introduces learners to observation, design failures, sketching, product thinking, technology trends and reflective documentation before regular classes begin.

### Idea Lab

A structured space for learners to develop early concepts, test assumptions, document iterations and convert observations into possible solutions.

### Project Display Room

A proposed environment where learner projects can be presented as evolving design stories rather than only as final products. The display approach emphasises the problem, process, evidence, prototype and learning.

## Operating approach

The programme-development work involves close coordination across course owners, faculty members, academic administrators and infrastructure teams. The emphasis remains on creating usable academic systems: lesson plans that teachers can execute, assessments that reveal learning, and project structures that students can navigate.

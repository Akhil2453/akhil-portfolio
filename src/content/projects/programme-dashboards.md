## Problem

Academic programmes generate large volumes of operational data: courses, units, assets, faculty assignments, review stages, deadlines, dependencies and quality statuses. Conventional spreadsheets often capture the data but do not make the decisions visible.

## Dashboard approach

The reporting design separates operational detail from review-level information. Stakeholders can begin with programme health, move into course or owner-level analysis, and then reach the underlying records requiring action.

## Portfolio development

Public demonstrations use simulated data so that the structure, calculations and data-storytelling approach can be showcased without exposing confidential institutional information.
